Great question! While the Arduino is plugged into the computer via USB, it is both receiving power and able to receive data (code uploads). Also, whatever other modules you have hooked up to the 5V pin of the Arduino is also getting power (the motor driver module). Once you unplug the Arduino from the computer, it stops getting power (although the code remains stored in the microcontroller).  The 12V 2.5A power supply you have connected to the motor driver module only powers the motor. The Arduino must be powered separately. It can be powered either through the USB port or the barrel jack right next to it. If your project doesn't need to move around you can use a separate 9-12V power adapter to power your Arduino. I've also used portable phone chargers to power the Arduino through the USB port.  Let me know how you make out!
