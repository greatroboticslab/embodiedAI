53:49 - the thing that needs to be understood is that an LED light turns on and off instantly. It said to full power as the filament in the bulb heats up and vice versa when you turn it off. Light emitting diode‚Äòs or LEDs turn on and off instantly. Great question!   They are very similar in some respects.  With a switch/case I generally think of it as testing a single variable, and then doing something specific based on that variables value.  Let's say you had a menu with a button, and you are counting buttons presses, and depending on the number of times you pressed the button (e.g. the button count), you do something different.  With an if/else, you can check multiple conditions, different variables, lots of flexibility.  Say you had a temp sensor, and you are doing different things based on different ranges of temperature perhaps, using if statement conditions to bracket your ranges.  In many cases you can accomplish the same thing using either, so it ends up being your decision as the develop on which to use.
